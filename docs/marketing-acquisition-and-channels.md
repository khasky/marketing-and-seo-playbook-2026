# Marketing I — Acquisition and channels

Launch sequencing, manual outbound, communities, lead magnets, email, directories, and social.

## App launch strategy

**Before launch**

- Build a leads list (waitlist, beta, content subscribers).

**At launch**

- Email the list with a clear CTA and founder story.
- Coordinate social pushes (see [Social](#social-media-posting--sharing)).
- Tie in directory and community tactics from the sections below.

## MVP / early versions sharing

**Goal:** testing and iteration.

Patterns:

- Start with your own network; expand to friends-of-friends.
- Private beta after a landing with screenshots — some teams take months to polish; adjust to your risk tolerance.
- Invite personal contacts to social channels to seed followers before public posts.

## How to find leads manually

- 1:1 conversations on LinkedIn, Facebook, niche sites.
- Guides: [45 ways to find clients from social](https://neshawoolery.com/blog/45-ways-to-find-clients-from-social-media), [Facebook groups lists](https://apollodigital.io/blog/best-facebook-groups).
- Founder content on Hacker News, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Indie Hackers (e.g. LunchMoney-style launch posts).
- Email finding: Hunter.io, Clearbit Connect, SalesQL plus NeverBounce-style verification.
- Twitter: see who follows competitors; [Followerwonk](https://followerwonk.com/) for exports; engage before pitching.
- Keyhole (or similar) for hashtag and campaign listening.
- Facebook Groups: give value first — answer questions, then share gated assets sparingly.
- Drafting technique: find authors who covered competitors; offer new data or angle.
- Twitter lists: segment ~100 influential accounts; list competitors without following if you prefer.

**LinkedIn email finder extensions** — search the Chrome Web Store for "LinkedIn email finder"; verify policies and ToS; many tools violate platform rules — prefer Sales Navigator plus official exports where possible.

## Chat advertise channels (Slack, Reddit, Quora, etc.)

**Goal:** traffic and direct leads without spam.

Mindset: value and conversation first, promotion second.

**Channels**

- Slack communities, Quora, Reddit, Product Hunt threads, Disqus on relevant blogs, forums, Facebook groups.

**Reddit**

- Read sub rules; disclose affiliation when required.
- Subs: `r/entrepreneur`, `r/SideProject`, `r/SmallBusiness`, `r/InternetIsBeautiful`, `r/design_critiques`, `r/startups`, `r/combin`, `r/programming`.

**Quora**

- Quality of traffic can shift as sales activity grows; some teams still win with deep answers and periodic updates.
- Suggested floor: ~20 thoughtful answers/month if Quora is a channel (illustrative — measure your own CPL).
- Anonymous Q+A games are discouraged; focus on real expertise.
- SEMrush-style advice: Quora can drive high-intent clicks if answers match intent and link contextually.

Niche communities (not only Quora) can yield strong organic reach if you participate authentically and share content where allowed.

Article: [RB.ru — "Look wider" (opinion piece on community channels)](https://rb.ru/opinion/smotrite-shire/).

## Email newsletter

**Goal:** convert leads to customers.

### Reading list

- [Buffer — email marketing Q&A](https://buffer.com/resources/email-marketing-10-most-important-questions-answered/)
- [Neil Patel — beginner guide](https://neilpatel.com/blog/beginners-guide-email-marketing/)
- [Habr — Pechkin on email and deliverability](https://habr.com/ru/companies/pechkin/articles/280510/)
- [Mail-Tester](https://www.mail-tester.com/) — spam/DKIM quick check

### Core steps

1. Permission (opt-in; double opt-in where required).
2. Open — subject line clarity; avoid spammy tokens ("Free," "Winner," "Click here," "This is not spam," etc. — context matters; mailbox providers use ML, not single words).
3. Read — scannable layout, one main idea.
4. Action — single primary CTA.

### Tactics

- Branded sending domain (e.g. Google Workspace on your domain).
- Cold email quality bar: would *you* open, read, reply?
- Tuesday open rates are often cited anecdotally — test your own audience.
- Welcome email — very high typical open rates; use for onboarding hooks.
- Emoji in subject — can help or hurt; A/B test.
- Signature link — low effort incremental visits.
- ~90% educational / ~10% promotional blend (rule of thumb).

### ESP pricing links (verify current)

ConvertKit, Brevo, SendPulse, Omnisend, Mailchimp, Mailgun, MailerSend, Mailjet — verify current pricing for RFPs.

## App launch directories

**Goal:** launch traffic.

Places: Product Hunt, GrowthHackers, Hacker News Show HN, [Uneed.best](https://www.uneed.best/), [Open Startup List](https://openstartuplist.com/), [Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/).

### Product Hunt

- One launch — treat it as a moment.
- Timing — ~12:00–12:30am PST is often cited for homepage competition; Tue–Thu often busy (tradeoff: audience vs noise).
- Assets — gallery, first comment, maker responsiveness.
- Email list primed to support within platform rules (no fake votes).
- 500 hunters-style lists — relationship-based asks, not manipulation.
- Reply to comments fast; absorb constructive critique.

Reads: [Medium — PH launch story](https://medium.com/startup-grind/how-i-launched-the-2-most-upvoted-product-of-all-time-on-product-hunt-f3772fb20ad8), [Welldoneby PH steps](https://welldoneby.com/blog/quick-steps-to-launch-on-producthunt-effectively/), [Foundr PH guide](https://foundr.com/articles/building-a-business/ecommerce/the-ultimate-guide-to-launching-on-product-hunt)

### Hacker News

- Strong if builders are users; Show HN can spike signups (e.g. LunchMoney-style spikes).
- Oversaturation means many posts get little traction — still cheap to try.

### Manipulation warning

Paid upvote services can create traffic without revenue and reputation damage. If coordinating friends, keep it transparent and within site rules.

## Social media posting and sharing

**Goal:** traffic plus SEO adjacency (brand SERPs, discovery, links where natural).

### Promotion checklist

- [Neil Patel — advanced promotion methods](https://neilpatel.com/blog/17-advanced-methods/)
- Best times: [CoSchedule](https://coschedule.com/social-media-marketing/best-times-to-post-on-social-media), [Sociality](https://sociality.io/blog/best-times-to-post-on-social-media/)
- Extensions: [SocialPilot Chrome extensions roundup](https://www.socialpilot.co/blog/best-chrome-extensions)

If long articles get no reads, distribution is the bottleneck: Facebook groups, Reddit (where allowed), HN, marketer communities (GrowthHackers, Indie Hackers), Quuu Promote, Slack groups, influencer shares, occasional ads.

### Content mix

- ~80–90% educate/entertain; ~10–20% direct promo (common consultant guidance).
- Recycling — social feeds move fast; repost evergreen with fresh hooks (attribute honestly).
- 24-hour half-life — on fast-moving feeds, plan for repost or stories so content does not disappear unseen.

### Platform cadence and content value

- Reach and mentions build recall; engagement (saves, comments) signals quality.
- Value types that perform in feeds: entertainment, knowledge, inspiration.
- Cadence differs by network: some operators believe Facebook may reduce organic reach if you post often with low-quality content; Instagram is often cited at 1–2 posts/day; X (Twitter) and VKontakte-style fast feeds can tolerate higher posting frequency.

### Twitter

- Republish winners with new copy after days/weeks.
- Hashtags — test; 1–2 often better than stuffing.
- Engage on target hashtags before broadcasting.

### Video

- Strong CTR on video across networks; short, clear beats long solicitations.
- LinkedIn native video historically strong for B2B reach (verify current algorithm notes).

### Scoop.it and curation

[Scoop.it](https://www.scoop.it/) — suggest your article to high-traffic topic pages for nofollow discovery traffic (Backlinko-style tip).

### Scheduling tools

Buffer-style per-network scheduling; Hootsuite reliability for Instagram is debated — test before committing. Also named: Eclincher, StoryChief, NapoleonCat, Kontentino, Postoplan, free tiers: [Greatly Social](https://inter.greatlysocial.com/index.php), [Publer](https://publer.io/plans).

## Paid search (SEM) — high-intent capture

Search ads (Google Ads, Microsoft Ads) target people actively querying — complementary to organic SEO and remarketing ([Paid media — remarketing](paid-media-remarketing.md)).

**Practice**

- Structure — campaigns by intent and geo; ad groups tight enough that ads match queries.
- Keywords — start with exact and phrase where possible; use broad only with negatives and monitoring.
- Negative keywords — maintain lists for jobs, free, DIY, competitor hiring, and irrelevant geography.
- Quality — ad relevance, expected CTR, and landing page experience feed ad rank; weak pages raise costs.
- Landing — dedicated URLs or clear sections on [pricing / features](landing-pages.md); message match between query, ad, and headline.
- Measurement — UTMs or auto-tagging; conversion actions in GA4 and ad platforms ([Measurement](measurement-attribution-and-experimentation.md)).

**Pairing** — capture demand with SEM; re-engage non-converters with remarketing; feed both with first-party lists where policy allows.
