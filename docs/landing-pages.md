# Landing pages and on-site conversion

Site structure and on-page pieces that support conversion, trust, and SEO. Pair with [Technical SEO](technical-seo-and-site-health.md) (canonicals, structured data, Core Web Vitals) and [Measurement](measurement-attribution-and-experimentation.md) for conversion tracking.

## Pricing and plans

**Goal:** move visitors to purchase.

Tactics:

- If visitors bounce, test custom pricing capture (popup or inline) with a strong magnet ("Tell us your volume — we'll tailor a plan").
- Scalable pricing — sliders or usage-based calculators reduce sticker shock.
- Say how you charge early — even pre-launch, capture emails from interested prospects so packaging matches demand.
- Flexibility copy — upgrade, downgrade, pause subscription (example pattern: SocialPilot-style messaging).

Example reference: [NapoleonCat pricing](https://napoleoncat.com/pricing/).

## Contact us

Google's quality rater docs have long pushed reachable sites: keep enough contact information and keep it consistent with domain/WHOIS signals where that applies.

One canonical contact page; include support paths (email, form, chat) you actually answer.

## About us and careers

**Goal:** show a credible team and a real hiring pipeline.

Suggested pages:

- About — mission, story, team, office or remote culture.
- Careers — open roles, hiring process.
- "We're hiring" strip or banner on key templates.

Investor angle: teams that ship often beat a mediocre v1; show advisors, accelerator participation, or relevant operator bios if you are early.

## Feature pages (programmatic SEO foundation)

**Goal:** traffic via detailed one-feature-per-URL pages.

Examples:

- [NapoleonCat — social inbox feature](https://napoleoncat.com/features/social-inbox/)
- [SocialPilot — analytics feature](https://www.socialpilot.co/social-media-analytics)

**Outline for each feature page**

- Outcome headline — what the user achieves.
- How it works — screenshots, short video.
- Integrations and limits.
- FAQ micro-block.
- CTA to trial or demo.

More on audience permutations: [Marketing II — FOR pages](marketing-pages-and-attribution.md).

## Comments in posts / blog comments

**Goal:** traffic and engagement signals.

### Disqus vs Facebook

- **Disqus** — richer threads; can attract spam without moderation.
- **Facebook comments** — historically weak for SEO when Google could not see the content; modern embeds may surface on your URL — verify implementation. Real-name friction can reduce participation.

### SEO angle

Pages with real discussion can correlate with engagement; quality and moderation matter more than raw count. A Googler once noted comments can help a lot when they add value.

### Lightweight alternatives

- [Cusdis](https://cusdis.com/)
- [HashOver](https://github.com/jacobwb/hashover) (self-hosted)
- [Hyvor Talk](https://talk.hyvor.com/)
- [GraphComment](https://graphcomment.com/en/pricing.html)
- [Super Dev Resources — comment feeds](https://superdevresources.com/comments/feed/)

## Blog newsletter popup

**Goal:** leads.

- Trigger at ~50% scroll or ~90% of post (near end of article body).
- Promise low frequency and relevant content ("1–2× per month on [topic] only").

Pair with double opt-in where regulations require it; see [Email — Marketing I](marketing-acquisition-and-channels.md).
