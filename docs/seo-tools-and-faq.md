# SEO — FAQ, keywords, and tools

Definitions, keyword strategy, and notes on common tools.

## FAQ — keyword metrics

**Keyword research tool** — suggests terms and shows CPC, volume, difficulty, and paid competition signals.

**CPC (cost per click)** — advertiser bid pressure; high CPC often means commercial intent.

**Search volume** — average monthly searches for a snapshot period; treat as directional.

**Ranking difficulty** — a score from your vendor (links on the SERP, intent, other factors). Compare within one tool, not across vendors.

**Paid competitors** — advertisers buying that query; hints at monetization potential.

## Keyword research principles

### Search intent

Before you chase keywords, search them and look at SERP types (blog vs product vs local). Match your page format to what people want.

### Long-tail keywords

Phrases with three or more words usually have clearer intent ("how to clean oak floors" vs "clean"). They often convert better even at lower volume.

### Volume vs qualification

A 1,000/mo long-tail with buying intent can beat a 50,000/mo head term with fuzzy intent — do not drop low-volume terms until you have checked intent and fit.
